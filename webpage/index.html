Saved by Sensors

A sensor solution for making girls interested in technology.

Overview

Interactive learning

Lost in Sensors is an interactive installation focusing on new technology. By showing primarily early-teen girls how the sensors in a smart phone works, and make them aware of the possibilities, it aims to anhance their interest in technology.

Background

Low interest in technology among girls

Today, many girls don’t get the technology skills required, and are lost behind the boys at the same age. This is a challenge in our society, and it is important to prioritize girls in relation to technology.

Science centers

Several museums have been established with a primary focus to bring technology and science closer to the general public. These museums, called science centers, often feature a more hands-on approach compared to classic museums.

Universeum exhibition

The largest science center in the Nordic countries is Universeum in Gothenburg, Sweden. A goal for Universeum the coming years is to make girls aged 12-14 more intereseted in the technological exhibitions and technology in general.

Process

Research

The team started out with researching what possibilities there are in sensors, and what had already been done in the field. Other exhibitions at Universeum were examined, to determina a fitting design for the new experience.

Rapid prototyping

THe technical aspects transformed from RFID-based to computer vision based, by the means of rapid hardware and software prototyping. Meanwhile, the designers of the team iterated the design using different materials in order to make the experience as interesting as possible, while comforming to what was techically possible.

Visuals

To catch the girls attention, a role model was chosen to create a narrative. As the installation would be located in the space area at Universeum, a Swedish-American astronaut named Jessica Meir were chosen as protagonist. She has a master’s degree in space research and has been trained as a NASA astronaut since.
